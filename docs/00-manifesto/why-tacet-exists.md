# Why Tacet exists

Count the apps you open to stay in touch with the people you actually care about.

Messages in one. Photos in another. A group chat somewhere else. The friend who
only posts long things. The one who only posts video. The professional face you
keep separate from the personal one. Seven apps, seven logins, seven inboxes,
seven algorithms — all to do one thing humans have always done: stay close to
their people.

Nobody chose this. It's what happened when every network decided the way to win
was to become a wall. Keep the people in. Keep the data in. Make leaving cost you
your friends. The walls worked — for the companies. For you, they turned one
simple need into a part-time job of maintenance and context-switching.

**That is the moment Tacet exists for:** the small, tired realization that you have
seven social apps to stay connected to the same handful of people, and that this is
absurd.

## What went wrong

The early internet had a good idea and forgot it. Email works because it is open.
You have one address. Your friends can be anywhere — a different provider, a
different company, a different country — and messages still arrive. Nobody thinks
about the protocol. It just works, across every wall, because there are no walls.

Social networking was supposed to be like that. It became the opposite. Instead of
one identity that travels, you got a new identity per platform, none of which you
own. Instead of your people, you got *the platform's* version of your people,
shown to you in *the platform's* order, interrupted by whatever the platform was
paid to interrupt you with. You stopped being the user and became the product.

## What Tacet is

Tacet is your home on the open social web.

One identity, that you own and can take with you. One place your people live,
whatever wall they happen to be behind. One calm window into the wider open web —
the Fediverse — where millions of people already talk to each other across
services the way email crosses providers.

Tacet is not another network to add to the seven. It is the home you plug the open
web into, so the seven can start to become one.

- **One identity** instead of a fractured self per app.
- **Your people** instead of an algorithm's guess at what will keep you scrolling.
- **No walls** — built on open protocols, so you're never locked in and can always
  leave with everything that's yours.

## The feeling we're chasing

Not excitement. Not novelty. Not the jittery dopamine of a feed that never ends.

**Calm.** The feeling of coming home. A place that is glad you're here and doesn't
mind when you leave. A place that shows you the people you love and then lets you
get on with your life. A place that was built for you, not for the metrics you
generate.

The internet forgot something. It forgot people. Tacet is the attempt to remember.

> Welcome home.
