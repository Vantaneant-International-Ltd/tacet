# Business model — how Tacet sustains itself

> **Status: living.** This is the public, plain-language statement of how Tacet is
> free, how Tacet is paid for, and what we will never do to make money. It contains no
> billing code and describes no product that is being charged for today. Where it names a
> price, that price is an intention, not an invoice. This is a public repository:
> everything written here is a public commitment.

Read [`FOUNDING_PRINCIPLES.md`](../../FOUNDING_PRINCIPLES.md) and the
[manifesto](../00-manifesto/) first. Tacet exists because the last generation of social
networks made you the product — ["you stopped being the user and became the
product"](../00-manifesto/why-tacet-exists.md). This document is how we make sure that
never happens here. The publishing side of the paid tiers is grounded in the
[publishing philosophy](../09-product/publishing-philosophy.md).

---

## Three things that are true at once

People sometimes assume "open source" and "a paid service" contradict each other. They do
not. Tacet is three separate things, and each one is true without weakening the others.

- **Open source.** The code is free. All of it. Anyone can read it, run it, fork it, and
  audit it. There is no open-core split where the real product lives behind a commercial
  licence.
- **Decentralised.** Nobody needs our permission to run a home. Tacet speaks the open
  protocols of the wider social web, so a home you host is a first-class citizen of the
  network — not a lesser copy that has to phone back to us.
- **The hosted service at [tacet.social](https://tacet.social).** Our servers, our
  bandwidth, our backups, our moderation, our labour. This is the version most people will
  use because they don't want to run a server, and paying for it is what sustains the
  project and everyone who works on it.

One does not contradict the others. The software being free is what makes leaving easy;
the service being good is why most people will stay. We would rather earn your stay than
lock your door.

## Free forever

These are commitments, not current promotions. We are stating them here, in public, so we
can be held to them.

**The software is free forever.**

- The full source is public and stays public.
- It is genuinely self-hostable — a home you run yourself is complete, not crippled.
- There are **no feature gates that exist only to upsell the hosted service.** If a
  capability lives in the open-source code, self-hosters get all of it. We will never
  reach into the software and remove something in order to sell it back on tacet.social.

**A genuinely usable free account on tacet.social is free forever.**

Not a trial. Not a teaser that becomes unusable in a month. The free account gives you:

- reading the open web — live across ActivityPub (Mastodon, Pixelfed, and the rest),
  RSS/Atom/JSON feeds, AT Protocol (Bluesky), and Nostr, all in one place (see
  [what Tacet reads today](../../README.md#what-tacet-reads-today));
- a home,
- a handle,
- fair limits.

Fair means fair: enough to actually live in, set by what keeps the service healthy, never
tightened as a lever to force an upgrade.

## The governing principle

> We price the service, never the software. We will never sell ads, never sell data, and
> never build engagement mechanics — these are not revenue paths we deprioritized; they
> are paths we reject.

This is the line everything else answers to. If a proposed way of making money requires an
ad, a data sale, or a mechanic engineered to keep you scrolling, it is not a trade-off to
weigh — it is already answered. (See the manifesto's
[anti-patterns](../00-manifesto/anti-patterns.md) and
[launch positioning](../07-brand/launch-positioning.md), which reject the same things from
the product and marketing side.)

Because the money comes from the service and not from you, we can show you the service's bill:
we publish [what Tacet costs to run](running-costs.md).

## Planned pricing

These are our intended prices as of the developer preview. Nothing is charged yet;
anything may change before billing exists, and founding members will never pay more than
what is listed here at the time they joined.

| Plan | Price | What it is |
|---|---|---|
| **Free** | €0 forever | Reading the open web, a home, a handle, fair limits. |
| **Tacet Plus** | €6 / month or €60 / year | Expanded sources, collections, full history; cross-posting when publishing ships. |
| **Identity add-on** | €24 / year | Custom-domain identity. Bundle: Plus + Identity €96 / year. |
| **Founding member** | €99 once, limited to the first 500 | Lifetime Plus + a permanent founding credit. Lighter patron option: €29 / year during the beta. |

### Managed Homes

For a group, a community, or an organization that wants its own home without running a
server.

| Home | Price | What it is |
|---|---|---|
| **Community** | €29 / month | About 100 members. |
| **Organization** | €99 / month | About 1,000 members, your own domain, moderation tools. |
| **Larger** | Custom, from €299 / month | Beyond that, sized to you. |

### Later, subject to change

Directions we may explore once the core is solid. Listed for honesty, not committed to, and
each still answerable to the governing principle above.

- **B2B presence tools** — for organizations maintaining a presence on the open web.
- **Paid curation** — a curator sets a price (€2–5 / month); Tacet takes a 20–30% share.
- **White-label licensing** — running Tacet under another brand, from €1,000 / month.
