# Communities

Group spaces around people and purpose. A supporting surface — not the spine.

Communities is the demoted successor to the old "rooms" model. It matters, but it deliberately sits below the five core surfaces.

## What it is

Places where a group of people gather around something shared — an interest, a project, a scene, a purpose. A community is a bounded space with its own people and its own conversation: a book club, a local group, a fan scene, a working team.

It's the part of the open web that is *collective* rather than one-to-one, given a calm home.

## Why it's a supporting surface, not the spine

The old "rooms" concept tried to make group spaces the center of gravity. We looked hard at that and demoted it on purpose.

The spine of Tacet is your [People](people.md) and your [identity](identity.md) — individual relationships and individual ownership. Communities are real and first-class in capability, but they are not the reason Tacet exists. Making group spaces the organizing idea would drift us toward "content in rooms" and away from [People before posts](../00-manifesto/why-tacet-exists.md). So Communities supports the core; it never becomes it. See the [information architecture](information-architecture.md).

## What it is NOT

- **Not the home of the product.** It supports People and Today; it doesn't replace them.
- **Not engagement-farming forums.** Not spaces tuned to maximize activity or manufacture drama for reach.
- **Not walled off from the open web.** A community is part of the Fediverse, not a private silo pretending the wider network doesn't exist.
- **Not a rebranded infinite feed with a group label on it.**

## How it behaves

**Around people or purpose.** Every community has a clear reason to exist and a clear set of people. If you can't say who it's for and why, it isn't one.

**Calm by construction.** The same rules that govern the rest of Tacet apply inside a community: bounded attention, legible ordering, no manipulation, no vanity scoreboards. A busy community should still feel like a good room, not a stampede.

**Human-scaled and governed.** Communities have people who tend them. Moderation and norms are the community's own, in the spirit of the open web — small, accountable, human, not algorithmic policing at planetary scale.

## How it relates to People and Today

Communities feed the core rather than competing with it. A community you care about can surface in [Today](today.md) as one more thing worth your attention — bounded, never a firehose. The people you meet in a community become part of your [People](people.md) if you choose, and you talk with them one-to-one in [Conversations](conversations.md).

A good community is a place you're glad to visit and glad to leave — where you find your people around a shared thing, and carry those relationships back into the rest of your Tacet.
