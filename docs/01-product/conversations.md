# Conversations

Correspondence, not anxiety.

Conversations is where you and your people actually talk — and it is deliberately built to replace the anxiety-based notification model that every other platform runs on.

## What it is

Your correspondence and presence in one calm surface: direct messages, replies, and mentions. The threads where real exchange happens. Things that are genuinely *for you* land here, and you attend to them when you choose.

Think of it as a well-kept inbox of human contact — closer to letters than to alerts.

## Why it exists

The notification center is the most manipulative part of modern social software. A red dot, a badge, a buzz: each is engineered to create a small itch of urgency so you'll open the app and stay. It trades your calm for their session numbers.

Conversations refuses that trade. [Calm before addiction](../00-manifesto/why-tacet-exists.md) means the things that matter still reach you — but as correspondence, on your terms, without a manufactured sense of emergency.

## What it is NOT

- **Not a notification center.** It is not a scrolling list of pokes engineered to pull you back.
- **Not red-dot manipulation.** No badge count designed to itch. No artificial urgency. No "you have 47 unseen things" pressure.
- **Not engagement bait.** "So-and-so liked your post" is not an event that earns an interruption. Vanity pings are retired legacy.
- **Not a feed.** Conversations is directed contact between people, not broadcast content.

## How it stays calm

**Correspondence, not alerts.** A reply or a message arrives the way a letter arrives — waiting for you, not chasing you. You decide when to read it.

**Signal, not noise.** Only things that are truly for you belong here: someone wrote to you, replied to you, mentioned you. Ambient activity does not generate a personal ping.

**No manufactured urgency.** No unread-count anxiety, no streaks, no "act now." If something is genuinely time-sensitive, it can say so honestly; nothing is dressed up as urgent to boost opens.

**Presence, gently.** Where presence is useful — seeing that a close person is around to talk — it's offered quietly and can be turned off. Presence should feel like a warm light in the window, never surveillance.

**You set the volume.** [Settings](settings.md) gives you real, generous calm controls: quiet hours, per-relationship preferences, and the ability to keep the whole surface silent until you come to it.

## How it connects

Conversations is fed by your [People](people.md) — the closer someone is, the more their contact matters — and it's reachable from [Today](today.md) and from any post via [Compose](compose.md). Because Tacet is on the open web, a message or reply works across platforms: talking to someone on another ActivityPub home feels exactly like talking to someone on Tacet.

The test for Conversations is whether staying reachable makes you feel connected rather than hunted. If opening it lowers your shoulders instead of raising them, it's doing its job.
