# People

The reason you're here.

People is the relationship layer of Tacet — your connections as first-class objects, not as a side effect of who you happened to interact with this week.

## What it is

A living view of the people you care about, wherever on the open web they live. The friend on Mastodon, the photographer on Pixelfed, the writer on WriteFreely, the account that posts things you love — all of them, in one place, held close.

People is where you go to *stay close*, deliberately. Not to grow a number. To keep your relationships warm.

## Why it exists

Because the people are the point. [People before posts](../00-manifesto/why-tacet-exists.md) and [Relationships before engagement](../00-manifesto/why-tacet-exists.md) are the first two principles, and they demand that relationships be a primary surface — designed, cared for, and central — not an afterthought computed from your engagement history.

Other platforms treat your relationships as fuel for a feed. Tacet treats the feed as fuel for your relationships.

## What you can follow

You follow **people, brands, and AI accounts** — all across the open social web. A follow is a relationship, not a subscription to content. Because Tacet lives on ActivityPub, it does not matter which platform someone is on; if they're on the open web, you can follow them and they can follow you. See [Discover](discover.md) for finding new people and [Identity](identity.md) for how your own account travels.

The protocol is invisible. Following someone on another server feels exactly like following someone on Tacet — the way emailing someone on another provider feels exactly like emailing someone on yours.

## What it is NOT

- **Not a follower-count scoreboard.** There is no vanity number to grow, no public tally that turns your relationships into a metric. Follower counts as status are retired legacy. Where a count is genuinely useful (managing who you follow), it is a private tool, never a trophy.
- **Not an audience-farming surface.** People is about closeness, not reach. Reach is not the goal here.
- **Not engagement-ranked.** Who's close to you is something you shape, not something an algorithm decides by measuring clicks.

## How it behaves

**Closeness is yours to shape.** You decide who your close people are. Tacet helps you keep those relationships warm — surfacing someone you haven't heard from, quietly, without guilt or gamified streaks.

**Finding your people.** Bring your world in from wherever they already are (see [Settings](settings.md) on portability and migration), and find new ones through [Discover](discover.md). Following is calm and reversible; unfollowing is quiet and carries no drama.

**Staying close.** People flows into [Today](today.md) — the people you're closest to are more likely to reach your entry point — and into [Conversations](conversations.md), where the actual talking happens.

The measure of success for People is simple and human: do you feel closer to the people you came here for? If yes, this surface is working.
