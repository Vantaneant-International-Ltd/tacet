# Product Vision

**Tacet is your home on the open social web.**

One identity you own. One place your people live. One window into the wider Fediverse. Calm by default.

## The problem: seven apps for the same people

Look at how a person actually stays in touch today. The same fifty people are scattered across seven apps. One friend posts photos on one platform, thoughts on another, video somewhere else. To keep up with people you already love, you install more software, accept more feeds, and absorb more manipulation designed to keep you scrolling past the person you came to see.

The apps are not built around your relationships. They are built around their engagement. You are the product; your people are the bait. Every one of them is a walled garden, and every wall exists so you cannot leave and cannot take your identity, your audience, or your history with you.

This is backwards. The people are real. The fragmentation is invented.

## The thesis: one home

The open social web already solved the hard part. ActivityPub lets independent platforms talk to each other, the way email servers do. A person on Mastodon can follow a person on Pixelfed can follow a person on PeerTube. No single company owns it. No wall is required.

What it lacks is a **home** — a calm, warm, coherent place a normal person would actually want to live in. The current on-ramps are built by and for people who enjoy configuring servers. That is a tiny audience. The open web deserves a front door built with the care of a great consumer product.

Tacet is that home.

- You get **one identity**, `@you@tacet.social`, that you own and can carry away.
- You get **one place** — organized around your [People](people.md), not around a feed.
- You get **one window** — [Discover](discover.md) — into everyone on the open web, wherever they live.
- And it stays **calm**: no infinite scroll designed to trap you, no red-dot manipulation, no vanity scoreboards.

Federation is plumbing. Users never need to understand it, the same way you never think about SMTP when you send an email. See the [manifesto](../00-manifesto/why-tacet-exists.md) and the [information architecture](information-architecture.md) for how this becomes a real product.

## Honest about reach

We do not lie about connection. Tacet is the best home for the **open** social web: Mastodon, Pixelfed, PeerTube, WriteFreely, Friendica, and other ActivityPub platforms.

It does **not** connect to closed platforms — Instagram, X, TikTok, LinkedIn, YouTube. Those are walled by choice. We name them only to describe the fragmentation problem, never as a promised integration. If one of them opens a real door someday, we will walk through it. Until then, we do not pretend.

## What winning looks like

We are not chasing time-on-app. We are chasing a feeling.

Winning is when a person opens Tacet and thinks:

- **I feel closer to my people.** The point of being here is the people, and it worked.
- **I feel calmer, not more anxious.** I left more settled than I arrived. Nothing manipulated me into staying.
- **This is mine.** My identity, my history, my audience — I own them, and I could leave with all of it tomorrow and take my people with me.

If we deliver those three feelings honestly, the metrics take care of themselves. If we ever have to choose between those feelings and a number going up, the feelings win. That is [Calm before addiction](../00-manifesto/why-tacet-exists.md) and [People before posts](../00-manifesto/why-tacet-exists.md), made concrete.
