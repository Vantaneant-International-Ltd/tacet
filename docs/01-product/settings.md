# Settings

Where you hold the controls — over your account, your privacy, your identity, and your calm.

Settings is a supporting surface. It sits at the edge, reached deliberately, but what lives here is load-bearing: this is where Tacet's promises become switches you actually hold.

## Account

The basics, kept simple: your credentials, your security, your sessions. Clear, honest, no dark patterns. Closing your account is a supported path, not a maze — the same principle as leaving with your identity intact (see [Identity](identity.md)).

## Privacy

Sane, protective defaults, and real control on top of them.

- **Audience defaults** — who your posts reach by default, easy to see and change. See [Compose](compose.md).
- **Who can reach you** — controls over messages, mentions, and follows, in the spirit of [Conversations](conversations.md).
- **Presence** — whether your being-around is visible, and to whom. Off is a first-class choice.
- **Data** — plain-language answers about what's stored and why. No harvesting hidden behind a profile.

We default to protecting you, not exposing you. Privacy is not a premium feature.

## Portability and export

The heart of [Identity before platforms](../00-manifesto/why-tacet-exists.md), made operational.

- **Export** — take your posts, profile, and data with you, in a usable form, whenever you want.
- **Migration** — move your identity to another home on the open web, and **bring your people with you**. Your connections follow; you don't start over.
- **Bring your world in** — the front door works both ways. Migrating *to* Tacet from another open-web home should be a warm, guided path, not a cold import.

Leaving is supported and honest. An owned identity you can walk away from keeps us accountable. See [Identity](identity.md).

## Federation identity

Your handle — `@you@tacet.social` — and the open-web reach behind it. Here you can see and manage how you appear across the Fediverse.

But federation stays invisible unless you go looking. It should feel like email: one address, many homes, the protocol out of sight. Nobody is required to understand ActivityPub to use Tacet. This section exists for the curious and the power user, never as a tax on everyone else. See [Discover](discover.md).

## Appearance

Make Tacet feel like yours. Calm, warm, and legible by default — Apple-quality warmth as a reference for craft, never a clone target.

- **Light and dark** — both first-class, honestly implemented, easy to switch, and able to follow your system.
- **Comfort** — type and density that stay cosy and unhurried, never cramped or stressful, especially on mobile.

## Notification calm controls

This is where [Calm before addiction](../00-manifesto/why-tacet-exists.md) becomes concrete, and it is deliberately generous.

- **Quiet by default** — you opt into interruption, not out of it. Nothing is tuned to pull you back.
- **Quiet hours** — real silence on your schedule.
- **Per-relationship volume** — hear more from your closest [People](people.md), less from everything else.
- **No red-dot machinery** — no manufactured urgency, no badge anxiety, no engagement pings. Vanity alerts are retired legacy. See [Conversations](conversations.md).

The rule across all of it: the person is in control, and the defaults protect their calm. If a setting ever exists to serve our numbers instead of your wellbeing, it doesn't belong here.
