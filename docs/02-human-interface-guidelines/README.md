# Human Interface Guidelines

This section is the bridge between what Tacet believes and what Tacet looks and
feels like. The [manifesto](../00-manifesto/README.md) sets the philosophy; the
[design system](../03-design-system/README.md) sets the tokens and components.
These guidelines are the layer in between: the interface principles that translate
the [five founding principles](../00-manifesto/founding-principles.md) into how the
product moves, spaces, reads, and behaves.

The governing rule still holds here: **the code follows the philosophy, not the
reverse.** When a UI convention is convenient but conflicts with a principle, the
convention is what's wrong.

## The core stance

Tacet is **"Your home on the open social web."** A home should feel like *yours* —
original, calm, rich, and alive. Those four words are the whole brief:

- **Original.** Tacet does not look like anything else, because it isn't anything
  else. It is not a feed with the serial numbers filed off.
- **Calm.** The interface never begs, pulses, or races. [Calm before
  addiction](../00-manifesto/founding-principles.md) is a designed property of
  every screen, not a mood we hope for.
- **Rich.** Calm is not thin. Tacet is warm, human, and full of life — people,
  faces, memory, texture. Restraint is not austerity.
- **Alive.** A home is inhabited. The product should feel like your people are in
  it, breathing, present — not like an empty content warehouse.

## The anti-clone stance (say it plainly)

Tacet must not be assembled from the parts of the products it exists to replace.

- **Do not copy X.** Not its icons, not its column, not its verbs, not its urgency.
- **Do not copy Instagram.** Not its grid, not its story rail, not its double-tap
  reflex.
- **Do not copy Mastodon.** We honor its protocol and reject its UI. Federation is
  invisible plumbing here, not a control panel.
- **Do not copy LinkedIn.** Not its density, not its dashboards, not its
  performance-of-self.
- **Do not copy Lifeinvader** or any parody-grade attention machine, literally or
  in spirit.

**Apple's Human Interface Guidelines are a quality reference — the standard of
care — not a clone target.** We borrow Apple's *discipline*: clarity, deference to
content, restraint, respect for the user. We do not borrow Apple's *look*, and we
never ship an iOS pastiche. Study the rigor; invent the surface.
