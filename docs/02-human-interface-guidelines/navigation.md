# Navigation

How a person moves through Tacet is itself a statement of values. The navigation is
not a menu of features; it is a map of a life. "Home Feed" is deliberately **not**
the core concept — a bottomless stream is the front door of the products we reject.

## The five places

The top-level navigation is five destinations, each answering a human question:

- **Today** — *What matters right now, calmly?* A bounded, restful view of what's
  new among your people. Present and useful like a window, never endless-by-design.
  It is not the app's gravity well; you can reach the end.
- **People** — *Who are my people, and how are we?* The relationship layer made
  first-class. People are easier to find here than any post. This is the heart of
  *People before posts*.
- **Discover** — *Who and what might I want to bring closer?* Gentle, opt-in
  widening of your world — never an algorithmic pull, never a trending scoreboard.
- **Conversations** — *Who is speaking to me?* Correspondence lives here. You learn
  someone reached out without being manipulated into opening the app. No red-badge
  urgency; this is the calm replacement for the notification casino.
- **Me** — *This is mine.* Your identity, your presence, your controls. One
  identity you own (`@you@tacet.social`), portable by design.

Supporting surfaces sit beside these, not above them: **Compose** (create quietly),
**Communities** (shared spaces), and **Settings**. They are reachable, not
insistent.

## Calm wayfinding

- **You always know where you are.** The current place is unmistakable. Movement
  between places is instant and predictable — no surprise modals, no interstitial
  pulls back to a feed.
- **Shallow, not deep.** Prefer a flat map of clear destinations over nested mazes.
  Every place is at most a step or two away from every other.
- **No hijacking.** Navigation never redirects your intent. Opening Conversations
  does not dump you into Today; finishing a task returns you where you were.
- **The app never begs.** There is no surface whose job is to keep you moving. When
  you're done, Tacet is happy to let you go.

## No red-badge nav

This is a hard rule. **No red count badges on navigation.** Red badges are
manufactured urgency — a variable-reward mechanic tuned to pull you back, and a
[named anti-pattern](../00-manifesto/anti-patterns.md).

Presence of *something new* may be shown with the calmest possible signal: a quiet
dot, a subtle weight change, a gentle "new since you were here." Never a number
counting up, never red, never anything that turns "you have correspondence" into
"you are behind." The goal is *awareness without anxiety*.

## Mobile and desktop shape

- **Mobile.** The five places live in a bottom bar — thumb-reachable, calm, cosy,
  never crowded. Mobile is always the more spacious of the two: lighter type,
  generous [spacing](spacing.md), one clear thing per screen. Never dense, never
  cramped, never stressful.
- **Desktop.** The five places live in a persistent side rail with room to breathe.
  Extra width buys whitespace and legibility, not more columns of content. Resist
  the urge to fill the canvas; a wide screen is not a mandate to pack it.
- **One mental model, two shapes.** The same five places, the same names, the same
  order everywhere. A person who learns Tacet on their phone already knows it on
  their laptop. See [typography](typography.md) and [motion](motion.md) for how
  transitions between places should feel.
