# Spacing

Whitespace is a feature in Tacet, not leftover margin. It is how the product
breathes, and breathing is what separates a home from a casino. Space is where
[calm](design-principles.md) actually lives.

## Density is the failure mode

The default instinct of social software is to fill every pixel — more posts, more
counts, more rails, more chances to keep you. Tacet inverts this. **Density is a
failure mode.** When a screen feels full, the fix is almost never "make it smaller
to fit more"; it is "show less."

This is [calm over dense](design-principles.md) made physical. A cramped screen is
a screen that has quietly started optimizing for throughput over people — the exact
trade the [principles](../00-manifesto/founding-principles.md) forbid.

## Rhythm

Space should feel intentional and consistent, like the measure in music.

- **A spacing scale, not arbitrary gaps.** Use a small, consistent set of spacing
  steps so rhythm is felt as order. Ad-hoc pixel values read as sloppiness.
- **Consistent vertical rhythm** down a screen — the eye should move in even,
  restful steps, never in jolts.
- **Group by space, not by lines.** Prefer whitespace to dividers and boxes for
  separating things. Boxes add density; space adds calm. Reach for a border only
  when space genuinely can't do the job.

## Breathing room around people and content

Because Tacet is [people over content chrome](design-principles.md), the things
that most deserve space are people and their moments.

- **Give people air.** A person's name, face, and your standing with them should sit
  in generous space, not be packed shoulder-to-shoulder with a dozen others. A
  crowded people list treats relationships like inventory.
- **Give a moment the frame.** A single note, photo, or conversation should be able
  to own the screen with room around it. Space signals importance; cramming signals
  supply.
- **Let the end be the end.** [Today](navigation.md) is bounded. Trailing whitespace
  and a clear stopping point are correct, not wasted — they say "you're caught up,"
  which is the opposite of the infinite feed.

## Mobile: always cosy

Mobile spacing is *more* generous than desktop, never less. Small screens tempt us
to compress; resist it.

- One clear thing per screen, with room around it.
- Comfortable tap targets with space between them — never a dense grid of tiny hits.
- Lighter, airier layouts over chunky, packed ones. Mobile should feel calm and
  cosy in the hand, never dense, cramped, or stressful.

## Where the specifics live

Set the philosophy here; keep the numbers in the
[design system](../03-design-system/README.md). The exact spacing scale, base unit,
and named tokens belong there so spacing stays consistent and the two documents
never disagree. This page is the *why*; the tokens are the *what*.
