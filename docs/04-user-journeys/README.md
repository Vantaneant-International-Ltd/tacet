# User Journeys

Principles are promises. These journeys are where the promises get kept — or don't.

Everything else in this documentation describes Tacet from the inside: what [Today](../01-product/today.md) is, what [People](../01-product/people.md) means, how [federation](../05-federation/README.md) works. This section describes Tacet from the outside, through the eyes of the one person we actually build for — someone who is tired of having seven apps for the same people, and hasn't quite let themselves hope there could be one calm home instead.

## How to read these

Read them as lived experience, not as specs. Each journey follows a real human through a real moment — what they feel, what they see, what the product quietly does, and which of our [founding principles](../00-manifesto/founding-principles.md) that moment is honoring or betraying.

They are a pressure test. If a principle sounds noble in the manifesto but produces a cold or anxious moment here, the principle is wrong, or we've built it wrong. Code follows philosophy — and philosophy has to survive contact with a human being on a Tuesday night.

## The arc

A relationship with Tacet has a shape, from the first curious glance to a graceful goodbye:

1. [First visit](first-visit.md) — the landing, and the "why do I have seven apps for the same people?" realization.
2. [Sign up](sign-up.md) — creating an account, calmly and honestly.
3. [Create your identity](create-identity.md) — choosing `@you@tacet.social` and understanding that you own it.
4. [Import your web](import-your-web.md) — bringing your existing online life in, honestly, without false promises.
5. [First follow](first-follow.md) — following your first person, including someone on another server.
6. [First conversation](first-conversation.md) — the first real exchange; presence without anxiety.
7. [First federation moment](first-federation-moment.md) — the quiet magic of the walls being gone.
8. [First week](first-week.md) — settling in; what a healthy relationship with Tacet feels like.
9. [Returning user](returning-user.md) — a bounded, finishable session you close satisfied.
10. [Leaving Tacet](leaving-tacet.md) — the most important trust journey: leaving, and taking everything with you.

## Why the arc ends where it does

Most products would never write the last chapter. We put it last on purpose. A home you cannot leave is not a home; it is a trap with nice lighting. [Open before closed](../00-manifesto/founding-principles.md) means the door is always unlocked — and the strange, quiet truth is that the unlocked door is exactly why people stay.
