# Create Your Identity

Now Maya picks who she is here. On most platforms this is a throwaway step — a username, a photo, next. On Tacet it's the foundation, because this identity is hers in a way none of her other accounts ever were.

## The handle

She types a name and it resolves into something that looks oddly familiar:

> **@maya@tacet.social**

An address. Not a username locked in a database she can never see, but an address shaped exactly like an email address — a name, an @, a home. And the resemblance is the point. When you email `maya@somewhere.com`, nobody on the other side needs to be on your provider. The address travels. So does this one. Her handle is how the entire open social web will find her and reach her — the friend on Mastodon, the photographer on Pixelfed, the writer on WriteFreely — all of them can address her here (see [first federation moment](first-federation-moment.md)).

## What "you own this" actually means

Tacet says it plainly, and means it:

- This identity is yours. Your handle, your profile, your posts, your list of people — they belong to you, not to us.
- You can take them with you. If you ever leave, you leave *with* your identity, not stripped of it (see [leaving Tacet](leaving-tacet.md)).
- We are your home, not your landlord holding your things hostage.

She's used to the opposite. Every other account she has is a tenancy that can end at the platform's whim, with her followers and her years of posts as collateral. Here, ownership isn't a marketing word. It's a design constraint we bound ourselves to.

## Setting it up

She adds a photo, a line about herself, maybe a link. Calm, minimal, no pressure to "complete your profile 34%." She can do more later or never. The profile is hers to shape, not a form to satisfy. See [Identity](../01-product/identity.md) for how the account itself is built.

## What she feels

A small, unexpected steadiness. For the first time, joining something doesn't feel like handing herself over. It feels like moving in.

## The principle in play

This is [Identity before platforms](../00-manifesto/founding-principles.md) made tangible. Her identity does not belong to the platform; the platform serves her identity. Every promise in this section — portability, reach, the graceful exit — grows from this one root: **you own who you are here.**
