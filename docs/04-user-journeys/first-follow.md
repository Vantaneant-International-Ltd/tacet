# First Follow

Maya wants to follow the photographer she's loved for years — the one whose work she used to open a certain app just to see. She heard he moved off Instagram to Pixelfed a while back and she never followed the trail. She half-expects this to be the moment Tacet gets complicated.

## The moment

She searches his handle. There he is: **@thomas@pixelfed.social**. Not on Tacet. On a different service entirely, one she's barely heard of.

She taps **Follow**.

It works. That's the whole event. No "this account is on an unsupported network." No bridge to configure, no protocol to learn, no warning that things might not sync. His photos begin arriving among her [People](../01-product/people.md), and if he glances at his followers he'll see `@maya@tacet.social` — a person on some server called Tacet — and it will mean nothing strange to him either.

## Why it feels ordinary (and should)

She's done this before without thinking, in a different medium. She has a Gmail address; her sister has one at a small company's own domain; her dentist uses something else entirely. She emails all three and never once considers which provider they're on. The address is enough. The letter arrives.

A follow across the open social web is that, exactly. `@maya@tacet.social` following `@thomas@pixelfed.social` is `you@gmail.com` writing to `them@theirjob.com`. Different homes, one open web, no seam. Tacet never makes her think about the machinery — [the protocol is invisible](../05-federation/README.md) by design.

## People, not content

And notice what she actually did: she didn't subscribe to a feed. She reconnected with a *person* she'd lost track of across the app-scatter. A follow on Tacet is a relationship, held in [People](../01-product/people.md) — someone she chose to keep close — not a line item in an engagement graph. The photos are lovely, but the point is Thomas, back within reach.

## What she feels

A small, satisfying click of *oh — it just works.* One of the seven apps just became unnecessary. The photographer she thought she'd have to chase across platforms is simply here now, because "here" turned out to mean the whole open web at once.

## The principle in play

This is [People before posts](../00-manifesto/founding-principles.md) resting on [Open before closed](../00-manifesto/founding-principles.md). The openness isn't the feature Maya notices; the *closeness* is. The openness is just what makes the closeness possible without walls.
