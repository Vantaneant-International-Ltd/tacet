# First Visit

Maya opens the link a friend sent her. She is not looking for another social network. She is, if anything, looking for the opposite — she just doesn't have a word for it yet.

## What she's carrying

On her phone right now: Instagram for the photographers she loves, WhatsApp for the group chats, one app for the writer whose essays she reads, another for the friend who moved abroad, X she keeps meaning to delete, a fifth thing for the hobby community, a sixth she forgot she installed. Seven apps. And here is the part that quietly bothers her: they're mostly the same people. The same handful of humans, scattered across seven walled yards, each one buzzing at her for attention.

She has never phrased it out loud, but she feels it: *why do I have seven apps for the same people?*

## What she sees in ten seconds

The page is calm. No cookie wall throwing itself at her, no autoplay video, no counter of how many people signed up in the last minute. There's space. It breathes the way [our interface](../02-human-interface-guidelines/README.md) is supposed to breathe.

One line, plainly true:

> **Your home on the open social web.** One identity you own. One place your people live. One calm window into everything.

Below it, unhurried, an honest picture of the thing she already feels — the scattered apps, the same faces in each — and then the relief on offer: not a new yard to tend, but one home the others can reach.

Within ten seconds she understands the most important thing, and it's not a feature. It's a category. **This is a home, not another app.** Nobody is trying to win her attention. Something is trying to give her a calm place to keep her people.

## What the product does — and doesn't

It doesn't ask her to sign up before she understands anything. It doesn't flash "10,000 people joined today." It doesn't promise to magically suck in her Instagram and X — because it can't, and it won't lie about it (see [import your web](import-your-web.md) for the honest version). It shows her what it actually is and trusts her to want it.

The only invitation is a quiet one: *make yourself at home.*

## The principle in play

This is [Calm before addiction](../00-manifesto/founding-principles.md) and [People before posts](../00-manifesto/founding-principles.md) meeting a stranger at the door. A first visit is the easiest place in the world to deploy a dark pattern. We don't. The first thing Tacet does for Maya is respect her — and that respect is the whole pitch.
