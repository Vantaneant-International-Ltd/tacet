# First Week

A week goes by. This is the stretch where most apps have already begun to change Maya — trained the twitch, installed the reflex to check. Tacet is trying to do something almost countercultural: earn a place in her life without taking it over.

## How the days actually go

**Monday.** She opens [Today](../01-product/today.md) over coffee. A few things worth seeing — Thomas's new photo, a reply waiting, an essay from the writer. She reads what she wants, and Today *ends.* It doesn't keep unspooling. She finishes it and goes to work, lighter, not later.

**Wednesday.** She doesn't open it at all. Nothing punishes her. No "you've missed 47 posts," no streak she's about to break, no guilt-badge waiting to shame her back. When she returns Thursday, her people are simply still there, held in [People](../01-product/people.md), exactly where she left them.

**Saturday.** A longer sit. A real conversation, a few new follows, someone she'd lost track of found again. She stays a while because she wants to, not because a mechanism won't let her leave.

## What's quietly missing

By day seven she notices what *isn't* happening. No 11pm doomscroll she can't climb out of. No phantom buzz in her pocket. No low static of comparison. No sense that some counter is judging her for how little she posted. The compulsion she assumed was just "how these apps feel" turns out to have been a design choice all along — one Tacet declined to make. See the [anti-patterns](../00-manifesto/anti-patterns.md) it refused.

The people, though, feel *closer.* Fewer apps, less noise, and somehow more of the actual humans. The scatter is healing. Three of the seven apps have gone quiet on her phone because their people live here now.

## What a healthy relationship looks like

Here's the strange, defining thing about the first week: **Tacet is glad when she leaves, and glad when she comes back.** It doesn't measure its worth in her minutes. A good week on Tacet might be twenty calm minutes total. That's not failure; that's the product working. It wants to be the calm home she returns to — not the habit she can't put down.

## What she feels

Settled. Un-frantic. A little surprised that a social product can sit lightly in a life instead of colonizing it. She's not "engaged." She's *close to her people,* and free. Those were supposed to be a trade. Here they aren't.

## The principle in play

This is [Calm before addiction](../00-manifesto/founding-principles.md) proven over time — the hardest place to prove it, because a week is long enough for dark patterns to show. A home you can leave, that welcomes you back without a grudge, is the whole thesis, lived.
