# Leaving Tacet

This is the most important journey in this whole section, and the one almost no product would dare write down. Someday, maybe, Maya wants to leave. This is where we prove that everything we told her was true.

## The test we set ourselves

Every promise Tacet made — *you own your identity, you own your people, we're your home not your landlord* — is just marketing until this exact moment. The day someone tries to walk out the door is the day you find out whether the door was ever real. Most platforms hang a beautiful door on a locked wall. We didn't. And the only way to know that is to watch someone leave.

## What she can take

Maya can leave, and take her world with her:

- **Her identity travels.** Her connections, her follow list, the people she's kept close in [People](../01-product/people.md) — she can move them to another home on the open web. Because her handle was always an [owned address](create-identity.md), not a rented username, moving is *moving,* not starting over from zero.
- **Her data is hers.** Her posts, her follows, her blocks — a real, portable export she can carry out. Not a screenshot. Not a hostage negotiation. Her own things, in her own hands.
- **Her people stay reachable.** This is the deepest part. Because the open web is one web, leaving Tacet for another Fediverse home doesn't sever her from Thomas on Pixelfed or the writer on WriteFreely. She changes homes; she doesn't lose her people. The [federation](../05-federation/README.md) that let her follow across walls also lets her *leave* across them.

## What Tacet refuses to do

No exit maze. No dark-pattern "are you *sure*? you'll lose everything!" designed to frighten her into staying. No holding her followers hostage. No slow-walking her export. No degrading her account on the way out to make leaving hurt. The [anti-patterns](../00-manifesto/anti-patterns.md) list is a fence, and it holds tightest here, where the temptation to trap is strongest.

When she goes, Tacet says something close to: *thank you for staying a while. Your things are yours. The door's open if you ever want to come back.* And means it.

## Why the open door is why they stay

Here is the quiet paradox at the center of Tacet. A person who knows they can leave — cleanly, whole, with everything — is a person who can trust the place. And trust is what makes a home feel like home. The locked-in user is a captive, and captives resent their captor and leave the instant they can. The free user is a *guest who chose to stay,* and chooses again tomorrow.

We don't retain Maya by trapping her. We keep her by being worth returning to, and by being brave enough to let her go.

## The principle in play

This is [Open before closed](../00-manifesto/founding-principles.md) at its most costly and most honest — the principle that only means anything when it's expensive to honor. A graceful departure isn't the failure of the relationship. It's the proof the relationship was ever real.
