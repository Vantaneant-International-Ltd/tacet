# Returning User

It's a month in now. Maya opens Tacet on a Tuesday evening with ten minutes before dinner. This is the ordinary session — not the first anything, just a normal return — and it's where a product's real character shows. What happens in these ten minutes is what Tacet actually *is* to her.

## What she opens into

[Today](../01-product/today.md). Not a home feed. Not an infinite river. A bounded, finishable answer to one honest question: *what's worth your attention right now?*

Tonight it's a handful of things — Thomas posted from a trip, the writer published something long she'll save for later, a conversation she was in has a warm new reply, a friend shared news she's genuinely glad to know. Maybe eight items. Not eight hundred.

## The thing that doesn't happen

She reaches the end.

That's it. That's the quiet revolution. She gets to the bottom and there *is* a bottom. No "you're all caught up!" that immediately loads more anyway. No related-content trapdoor. No autoplay pulling her into a next thing she never chose. Today respects that attention is finite and hands her a finite amount of it, then steps back.

She isn't fighting an undertow. There's nothing to resist, because nothing is pulling. The [design](../02-human-interface-guidelines/README.md) is built to *release* her, not retain her — the rarest thing a social surface can be built to do.

## Ten minutes, well spent

She reads three things properly. Replies to the warm one. Taps to save the essay. Glances at [People](../01-product/people.md) to see if anyone she's close to has surfaced. And she's done — not interrupted, not torn away, *done.* The session had a natural end and she met it.

Dinner's ready. She closes the app.

## What she feels

Satisfied, and slightly full — in the good way, the way a good short conversation leaves you, not the hollow over-stuffed way an hour of scrolling leaves you. She got what she came for and nothing tried to keep her past it. She'll be back tomorrow because tonight was calm, not because a hook is set.

## The principle in play

This is [Calm before addiction](../00-manifesto/founding-principles.md) at its most concrete: **bounded and finishable.** No infinite scroll, no retention trap, no manufactured "one more." A returning session that ends cleanly is the promise kept a hundred times over — and it's why the returns keep coming, freely.
