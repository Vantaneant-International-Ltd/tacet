# Sign Up

Maya decides to try it. This is the moment most products treat as a funnel — a place to extract, to pressure, to trick. It's the moment Tacet treats as the beginning of a relationship.

## What she expects

She braces for the usual: a form that asks for everything, a demand to connect her contacts "to find her friends," an upsell before she's even inside, a pre-checked box quietly opting her into email she'll spend a year unsubscribing from. She expects to feel slightly used by the time she's done.

## What actually happens

It's short. Almost suspiciously short.

She gives an email or picks a passkey. She chooses her handle (that's its own moment — see [create your identity](create-identity.md)). That's essentially it. No contact-book harvest. No "invite five friends to continue." No personality quiz to feed a recommendation engine. No pre-ticked consent.

Every optional thing is visibly optional and honestly labeled. If Tacet asks for something, it says why in plain language, and "not now" is always a real button, never a grey ghost she has to hunt for.

There is no artificial scarcity — no countdown, no "your spot expires in 4:59." There is no fake momentum — no fabricated notification that someone is waiting for her. The calm from the [first visit](first-visit.md) doesn't evaporate the moment she's a lead. It stays.

## What she feels

Relief, and a small surprise. She got *in* faster than she expected and gave away *less* than she feared. Nobody tried to manipulate the moment. She notices its absence the way you notice a room going quiet — she hadn't realized how loud the usual sign-up was until it wasn't there.

## What the product does — and doesn't

It sets up the one thing that matters — an [identity she owns](create-identity.md) — and defers everything else until she wants it. It does not front-load friction to inflate a completion metric, and it does not front-load extraction to inflate a data pile. It gets out of her way.

## The principle in play

This is [Calm before addiction](../00-manifesto/founding-principles.md) enforced at the exact point where it's most tempting to break it, and it's the manifesto's [anti-patterns](../00-manifesto/anti-patterns.md) list held as a promise: no dark-pattern funnels, no manufactured urgency, no coerced consent. A sign-up is a handshake. We keep ours honest.
