# Portability

**Open before closed, made real.** Portability is the promise that your identity and your relationships are *yours* — not borrowed from a platform that can revoke them. It is, in the end, the most honest thing Tacet can offer, because it means we have to earn you staying rather than trap you into it.

## What you own

On the open social web, your identity is a real, portable thing:

- **Your handle and identity** — `@you@tacet.social` is a home on the open web, addressable by anyone, in the same way an email address is yours to use anywhere.
- **Your relationships** — the people you follow and who follow you are a graph you can carry, not a list the platform holds hostage.
- **Your content and history** — what you've posted and gathered is exportable, not sealed inside a vault only we hold the key to.

## Moving your identity — and taking your people

Because the protocol is open, leaving doesn't mean losing. If you move to another home:

- **Your handle can move with you**, and, where servers support it, your followers are carried across so the people who follow you keep following the new you.
- **You can take your world.** You leave *with* your relationships and your history, not empty-handed. You don't rebuild your life from zero on the way out.
- **You can arrive the same way.** Someone moving *to* Tacet from another home can bring their people and history in — the front door works both directions.

The mechanics of moving accounts, redirecting a handle, and migrating followers live in the [engineering adapter](../06-engineering/activitypub-adapter.md). Here, the meaning: you are never locked in.

## Leaving without losing your world

The old social bargain was: build your life here, and if you leave, it dies. That's the walled garden's real power — not that it's good, but that leaving is punishing. Tacet refuses that bargain. You should be able to walk out the door with your identity, your people, and your history intact, and we should be fine with that. If you stay, it's because Tacet is a good home — not because escape was made too costly. See where this sits among the [founding principles](../00-manifesto/founding-principles.md).

## Why portability is the ultimate honesty

Every other promise in this section — invisible protocol, faithful remote content, calm discovery — asks you to trust us. Portability is the one that proves the trust is safe, because it hands you the exit key up front. A company that lets you leave freely is telling the truth about wanting you to stay for the right reasons. That's why portability isn't a feature bolted on at the end. It's the point:

> **You own your home. If we stop being a good one, you can leave and keep everything that mattered.**

That's Open before closed. That's the whole bet.
