# Copywriting

How the [voice](voice-and-tone.md) becomes real words on real screens. Two rules
sit above all others: **lead with what Tacet is**, and **never be dishonest or
manipulative.**

## General guidance

- Short lines. Read every sentence aloud; if you run out of breath, cut it.
- One idea per line. Don't stack clauses.
- Plain words over clever ones. Never explain the joke.
- Positive framing. Say what a thing does, not what it avoids.
- No dark patterns: no fake scarcity, no guilt, no "are you sure you want to leave?" manipulation.

## Headlines

Lead with the person and their people, not the technology.

- **Do:** "Your people, in one place."
- **Do:** "One home on the open social web."
- **Don't:** "A federated ActivityPub client with no algorithm."
- **Don't:** "The last social app you'll ever need!"

## Buttons and labels

Verbs, plain and specific. No filler.

- **Do:** `Follow` · `Reply` · `Find your people` · `Post` · `Save`
- **Don't:** `Submit` · `Click here` · `Let's go!` · `Engage`

## Empty states

An empty screen is an invitation, warmly. Never a scold, never pushy.

- **Do (People):** "No one here yet. Bring your world — search a name or paste an address like `@friend@mastodon.social`."
- **Do (Today):** "Quiet so far. When your people post, you'll see it here."
- **Don't:** "You have 0 followers. Invite friends now to grow your network!"

## Error messages

Say what happened, then what to do. Calm, never cute, never blaming the user.

- **Do:** "That address didn't resolve. Check the spelling — it looks like `@name@server`."
- **Do:** "Couldn't reach that server just now. We'll keep trying."
- **Don't:** "Oops! Something went wrong 🙃"
- **Don't:** "Invalid input. Error 422."

## Before / after

| Before | After |
| --- | --- |
| "Join the social revolution!" | "Your home on the open social web." |
| "No ads, no algorithm, no tracking." | "Your feed, in the order things happened." |
| "Connect all your networks." | "Follow anyone across the open web." |
| "Don't miss out on updates!" | "New from your people since you left." |
| "Sync with Instagram and X." | *(cut — we can't, so we don't say it)* |

The last row is the honesty rule in practice. We never write copy that promises
something we don't build. See [launch positioning](launch-positioning.md) for
what we will and won't claim.
