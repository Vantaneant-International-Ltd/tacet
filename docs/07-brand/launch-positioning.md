# Launch positioning

How we tell the world what Tacet is, on day one.

## The problem we name

You have seven apps for the same people. The group chat is on one, your
photographer friend posts on another, the person whose writing you love is on a
third, and the running club lives somewhere else entirely. Same faces, scattered
across a screen full of icons — each one designed to keep you scrolling.

The feeling we speak to: *"Why do I have seven apps for the same people?"*

We name closed platforms (Instagram, X, TikTok, LinkedIn, YouTube) **only** to
describe this fragmentation. That is the extent of it. We never imply we connect
to them.

## The promise

**One calm home for your people.**

- **One identity you own** — an address that travels the open web, like email. `@you@tacet.social`.
- **Your people in one place** — the same faces, no longer scattered.
- **A calm window into the wider Fediverse** — read, follow, and reply across the open web from one home.

The relief on the other side of the problem: instead of seven apps pulling at
you, one place that feels like home and lets you breathe.

## Honest open-web framing

Tacet is the best home for the **open** social web:

- Mastodon
- Pixelfed
- PeerTube
- WriteFreely
- Friendica

These speak a shared language (ActivityPub — see [federation](../05-federation/README.md)),
and Tacet gives them one warm front door. We describe federation as **email**:
one address, many homes, the protocol invisible.

## Who it's for

People who are tired of the attention economy and want their relationships back.
Not a niche of protocol enthusiasts — anyone who feels the seven-apps fatigue and
wants somewhere calm to keep their people. The Fediverse-curious are welcome; the
Fediverse-unaware should feel just as at home, because we hide the plumbing.

## The stance: positive, never "we cut X"

We lead with what Tacet **is**. The things we refuse — ads, engagement
algorithms, manipulation — are real and they matter, but they are the fine print,
not the pitch. A home is described by who lives there, not by what you kept out.

- **Lead:** your people, your identity, your calm home on the open web.
- **Support (quietly):** in the order things happened; no ads; nothing engineered to keep you scrolling.

## What we will and won't claim

**Will:**

- "Your home on the open social web."
- "One address for every home on the open web."
- "Follow and reply to people across the Fediverse."
- "The best home for Mastodon, Pixelfed, and the rest of the open web."

**Won't:**

- Any claim of integration with Instagram, X, TikTok, LinkedIn, or YouTube.
- "Connects to every network."
- Federation features that aren't built yet stated as present tense — see the [roadmap](../08-roadmap/README.md) for the honest sequence.
