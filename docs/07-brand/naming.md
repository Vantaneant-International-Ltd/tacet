# The name: Tacet

## Meaning

*Tacet* is a term from written music — Latin for "it is silent." On a score it
tells a performer that their instrument rests: play nothing here. Not an absence
of music, but a deliberate, composed quiet. Space held on purpose.

That is the whole idea. Tacet is the opposite of noise. Where other products are
engineered to keep you playing — more notifications, more feed, more pull — Tacet
is the rest in the bar. The room to hear the people who matter.

## Why it fits

- **Calm before addiction.** The name is a promise about attention, not a feature list.
- **Quiet is a choice, not a lack.** A rest is written into the music. Our restraint is composed, deliberate, and confident — not thin or empty.
- **It reads well spoken and typed.** Two syllables, easy to say, easy to trust. It does not shout, which is the point.

It is a warm quiet, not an austere one — the hush of a room where your people
already are, not an empty hall.

## The domain and address

The product lives at **`tacet.social`**.

Your identity is an address you own:

```
@you@tacet.social
```

One address, many homes, protocol invisible — the way email works. See
[federation](../05-federation/README.md) and [identity](../01-product/identity.md).
The `@handle@server` shape is the Fediverse convention; we treat it as an
address, never as jargon to explain to a newcomer.

## VNTA Group context

Tacet is a venture of the **VNTA Group** (Vantanéant International). It follows
VNTA's family instinct for restraint and honesty, but Tacet has its own warm,
human voice — see [voice-and-tone](voice-and-tone.md). Reference the parent as
"a VNTA Group venture," lightly, when context calls for it. It is not a headline.

## Usage and capitalization

- Write it **Tacet** — initial capital, rest lowercase. Never TACET, never tacet in prose, never "the Tacet."
- It is a proper noun. "Tacet is your home on the open social web," not "a Tacet account is..." (prefer "your Tacet account").
- The domain and handle are always lowercase: `tacet.social`, `@you@tacet.social`.
- Do not italicize the name in product copy. Italics are fine only when referring to the musical term itself, as at the top of this page.
- Never explain the pun in-product. The name earns itself; a footnote would undo it.
