Do NOT implement this now.

This document defines the future Product UX milestone for Tacet.

It should only be executed once the core product exists.

The purpose of this milestone is NOT visual polish.

It is NOT a redesign.

It is NOT about colours, gradients or animations.

It is about making Tacet feel inevitable.

Every interaction should feel like there was never another way to design it.

---

# Philosophy

Treat this as if you were:

Senior Product Designer

Staff UX Designer

Apple Human Interface Team

Linear Product Team

Arc Browser Design Team

Notion Product Team

Do not imitate any of them.

Use them only as a quality bar.

The result must feel uniquely Tacet.

---

# Goal

Review the ENTIRE product.

Not individual screens.

The entire experience.

Pretend this is six months before public beta.

The backend is stable.

The architecture is stable.

The product exists.

Now ask:

"Does this feel like one coherent product?"

---

# Question every interaction

Should this button exist?

Should this be one click?

Two clicks?

Is this page necessary?

Can this become a sheet?

Should this be inline?

Should this be progressive disclosure?

Can we remove this completely?

Simplify relentlessly.

---

# Product rules

Never expose protocol concepts.

Never expose ActivityPub.

Never expose AT Protocol.

Never expose federation.

Never expose server terminology.

Never expose implementation details.

Users should think:

"I am using Tacet."

Never:

"I am using Mastodon."

---

# Composer

Review the publishing experience.

Do NOT think in terms of:

Twitter post

Instagram post

LinkedIn post

Reddit post

Instead think:

Thought

Photo

Video

Article

Event

Question

Everything begins with intent.

Not destination.

---

# Profiles

Design the distinction between:

My private home

My public profile

Remote profiles

Business workspaces

Personal workspaces

These should feel natural.

---

# Navigation

Review whether every destination belongs.

Remove anything unnecessary.

The app should feel calm.

Not feature rich.

---

# Home

Today should feel like:

Good morning.

Here is your world.

When you reach the end,

you are finished.

No endless doomscrolling.

---

# Me

Should feel like:

Walking into your own home.

Not opening settings.

---

# Search

Should search:

People

Posts

Collections

Saved

Notes

Eventually everything.

Without becoming overwhelming.

---

# Metrics

Treat metrics as context.

Never as rewards.

No dopamine.

No gamification.

---

# Notifications

Should be calm.

Grouped.

Intentional.

Never addictive.

---

# Business workspaces

Should feel like:

Switching Notion workspaces.

Not logging out.

---
